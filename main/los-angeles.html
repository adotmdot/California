The Year in Los Angeles was Incorporated

Los Angeles was officially incorporated as a city on April 4, 1850. The city's origins trace back much earlier, to September 4, 1781, when a group of 44 settlers, known as "Los Pobladores," established the original settlement under Spanish rule. During the early 19th century, Los Angeles remained a small town under Spanish and later Mexican rule. However, in 1848, after the Mexican-American War, California became part of the United States through the Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo. Just two years later, in 1850, Los Angeles officially became a city, coinciding with California's admission as the 31st state of the U.S.

The incorporation of Los Angeles marked the beginning of its transformation from a modest pueblo into a rapidly growing metropolis. Throughout the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the city expanded significantly due to the arrival of railroads, the discovery of oil, and its development as a major economic and cultural hub. Today, Los Angeles stands as the second-largest city in the United States, known worldwide for its entertainment industry, diverse population, and economic influence.

Region of the State Where Los Angeles is Located

Los Angeles is located in Southern California (SoCal), along the Pacific Coast of the United States. It is situated in Los Angeles County and is bordered by the Pacific Ocean to the west, the San Gabriel Mountains to the north, and the deserts of California to the east. The city is part of the Greater Los Angeles metropolitan area, which extends into neighboring counties like Orange, San Bernardino, Riverside, and Ventura.
